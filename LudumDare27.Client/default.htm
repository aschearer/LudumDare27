The Story of Duel City

Welcome to Duel City. The weather is fair, the ladies are pretty, and our diet is high in lead. You probably heard some yelling when you arrived, that would be Sally Sissypants and Riley Roundbottom. They are always getting into arguments over the most trivial of things: missing steps during the square dance, overcooking vegetables, extra-marital affairs, century-long blood feuds, etc.

I wouldn't be surprised if one of them asked you to be their second. You don't know what a second is?!? Well, fry me in peanut butter! Listen up, there are four parts to a duel: the challenge, determining the location, the ten paces, and the aftermath.

  1. The Challenge: Those two knuckleheads are always challenging each other. Why, just this morning I heard Sally challenge Riley because Riley's underwear was too yellow. Now, how did Sally even know that?
  2. The Location: As a second, this will be your most important responsibility. You should choose terrain that benefits your duelist. Why not one full of traps? Duel City is littered with traps, so there shouldn't be any problem finding some. Just make sure to remember which traps you've used!
  3. The Ten Paces: The duelists take ten paces, turning and firing after each step. Ten trap filled paces. That's where you come in: advise the duelist about the traps they should expect.
  4. The Aftermath: If all goes well, honor is restored and drinks flow freely. Don't dwell on the alternative: the alcohol doesn't taste as sweet.

Three Tips to be First among the Seconds

Having trouble keeping your duelist alive? One too many holes spoiling your afternoon plans? Buy the complete guide to duel-assistance for four easy payments of $9.99! Be the envy of your neighbors, grow taller and more handsome, impress your friends! "Seconds for Dummies" is chocked full of helpful tips like these:

  1. Always remember what traps were available at the start of the duel
  2. When choosing to remove traps, try to remove a class of traps from the duel altogether
  3. Keep track of which traps have been sprung over the course of the duel
